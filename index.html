Frequently Asked Questions

The most common traditional land measurement units in Nepal include Bigga, Kattha, Dhur, Ropani, and Anna. These units vary by region and are still widely used alongside modern metric measurements.
Our conversions are based on officially recognized measurement standards and are accurate to two decimal places. However, please note that local variations may exist in some regions.
Digital land measurement tools offer greater precision and efficiency compared to traditional methods. They reduce human error and provide instant results with GPS integration and modern surveying technology.
A Bigga is equivalent to 20 Kattha. While Kattha is commonly used for smaller land measurements, Bigga is typically used for larger agricultural lands and property measurements.
While our converter provides accurate calculations, it's recommended to consult with legal authorities or licensed surveyors for official documentation. This tool is meant for reference and preliminary calculations.
